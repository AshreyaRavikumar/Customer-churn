Business Understanding

This project addresses two critical questions for the subscription-based business:

Problem 1: CLV Prediction

Forecast the total value each customer will generate over their lifetime. This enables resource allocation, personalized marketing, and identification of premium customers.

Problem 2: Churn Prediction

Identify customers likely to cancel within the next 30 days. Early detection allows proactive retention interventions, reducing customer acquisition costs.

Business Impact: Improved retention ROI, optimized pricing, reduced CAC, and increased overall customer equity.

Key Insights

CLV Drivers

The most important factors for CLV are tenure (customer loyalty duration), monetary value (total spend), and plan type. Premium and Enterprise customers contribute significantly higher lifetime value. Engagement metrics (active days, events per day) also strongly correlate with higher CLV.

Churn Risk Factors

High churn is associated with low engagement (few events/transactions), high recency (inactive for >30 days), poor support experiences (low satisfaction scores, long resolution times), and Basic/Standard plan tiers. Customers in the first 6 months have the highest churn risk.

Segment Differentiation

K-Means clustering identified 4 distinct customer segments with varying CLV and churn profiles. Segment 0 (high-value loyal) has avg CLV of $3,503 with 0% churn, while Segment 3 (low-value at-risk) has avg CLV of $342 with 27.3% churn rate.

Support Quality Impact

Satisfaction score and resolution time are strongly correlated with both CLV and churn. Customers reporting low satisfaction (<3) are over 3x more likely to churn, highlighting the importance of support quality in retention.

Refund Behavior

Customers with multiple refunds tend to have significantly lower CLV and higher churn probability, making refund patterns an early warning signal for churn risk.

Actionable Recommendations

1. Proactive Retention for High-Risk Customers

Trigger personalized outreach when churn probability exceeds 60%. Offer targeted discounts or plan upgrades to high-value at-risk customers. Implement a win-back campaign for customers showing disengagement signals (e.g., no activity for 14+ days).

2. Onboarding Optimization

Focus retention efforts on the first 90 days (highest churn period). Implement guided onboarding with milestone rewards. Monitor early engagement as a leading indicator of long-term retention.

3. Support Experience Enhancement

Reduce resolution time for high-value customers. Implement satisfaction follow-ups for scores below 3. Route churn-risk customers to specialized support teams equipped to address their concerns.

4. Plan Optimization

Create incentives for Basic plan customers to upgrade to Standard. Introduce mid-tier plans with features that drive engagement. Offer loyalty discounts for customers approaching key tenure milestones (1 year, 2 years).

5. Data-Driven Targeting

Use predicted CLV to prioritize sales and marketing spend. Segment customers by churn risk for tailored email campaigns. Build automated alerts for sudden drops in engagement metrics.

6. Monitoring Dashboard

Track key KPIs: churn rate, CLV trend, segment movement. Monitor early warning signals: recency increase, ticket frequency, satisfaction decline. Provide weekly reporting of high-risk customer list to the retention team.
